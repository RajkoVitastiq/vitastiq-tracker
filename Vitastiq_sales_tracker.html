How COGS works

COGS (Cost of Goods Sold) is the direct cost of producing or acquiring the product you sold.

When you open + New Entry and select a model, the COGS field is automatically pre-filled with the value from here × quantity.

You can always override it manually for any individual entry — for example, bulk orders with different procurement costs.

⚠ Changing COGS here only affects future new entries. Existing entries keep their original COGS value.

To change COGS on an existing entry, click the edit (✎) button in the Sales Log.

COGS history — note
Each saved entry stores its own COGS value at the time of entry. This means historical gross profit figures remain accurate even after you update the default COGS here.

Example: If you sold 10 units at COGS €50 in January, and then updated COGS to €60 in March, the January entries still show €50 COGS and €208 gross profit per unit. March entries will use the new €60.
